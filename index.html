August Jobs Numbers Show Highest Quit-Rate on Record

By Dillon Kondor

October 14, 2021

The latest jobs numbers show the highest number of people quitting their jobs ever recorded.

While a certain amount of churn in the economy is normal, this week's Job Openings and Labor Turnover Summary (JOLTS) report shows that 2.9% of jobs in the economy were quit, the highest percentage ever recorded. 2021 was the first year that number went above 2.4 percent since the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics began keeping track in the year 2000.

These are the rates at which employees voluntarily quit their jobs in August, divided by industry.

Service Industry

6.8%

Employees at restaurants, bars, and hotels.

Retail trade

4.7%

Sales, customer service, and management of same.

Arts, entertainment, and recreation

3.7%

Theater, arts, music, and other entertainment jobs

Professional and business

3.4%

Office and administrative work in both the private and public sectors

Health care and social assistance

2.7%

Doctors, nurses, social workers, healthcare administrators

Manufacturing

2.5%

Factory and supply-chain jobs

A cursory glance at the numbers shows that the most-quit jobs tend to involve dealing with other people. It's possible that many of the workers in question no longer have the patience to deal with customers who quibble with inconsistent and spottily enforced mask and vaccine mandates. Or that a year in quarantine has worn down their patience for being spoken down to in general. Or, perhaps the pandemic just gave them an opportunity to rethink what they want to do with their time and energy, which is likely also true for many employees who quit non-public-facing jobs.
